Shourabi, M., & Noori, H. (2021). Design Space Exploration based on deep learning and hardware performance counters to predict the optimal configuration of a soft-core processor.

Abstract

Click on this to see abstract.

By the ever-increasing complexity of hardware processing architectures, CAD tools, and low-level simulation techniques no more can address the design gap issue. So, the need for high-level prediction will be more tangible. This paper presents a methodology to help designers find the optimum energy-consuming configuration of a softcore processor at a glance and with appreciable accuracy. The proposed framework extracts the integrated performance counters information within a softcore. Then, it uses deep learning to find a relation between the execution time of a given application and the hardware configuration. Using integrated performance counters can highly reduce intrinsic errors of simulation methods due to being tightly coupled to the hardware. By combining the performance estimation technique with static power information of the hardware, we proposed OEPM, a model that can offer the best configuration of softcore in terms of energy consumption for a given C source code. Our results reach the mean prediction error rates of below 1.5% for unseen applications
