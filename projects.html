Academic

Using deep learning to detect ophthalmic diseases, 2021

Traditionally, ophthalmologists had to take images from patients' eye with specific instruments, and analyze those images manually to detect any possible anomaly in the eye. The goal of project was to exploit deep learning methods to do the detection phase automatically. That is deep layers of neurons will be trained to classify images obtained from ophthalmology instruments. The results show that a well tuned deep network along with a large enough dataset can accurately detect the anomalies. This model in turn can be used in smartphones to help opticians in the disorder detection.

Android system modifying to filter external devices, 2020

A modification on the android external device management, a.k.a Volume Manager or VOLD, performed to authorize devices that are introduced privously to the system. Authorized devices must be added in a whitelist database by the admin. Unauthorized devices couldn't create device files in the target filesystem. This modification could be useful for customized android systems in critical infrastructures such as vehicles or building management systems that might be vulnerable to malicious external devices.
